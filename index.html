About Me

My name is Stephanie Soules and my life revolves around my French Bulldog named Violet Mae and humanitarian work. Before Covid-19 Violet and were out in the community daily. She is a Pawlitical Council Member and our community is very important to us and we are always working to make it a bit better each day.

Humanitarian Work

My number one passion in life is Humanitarian work. I inherited an 8-foot by 22-foot custom built BBQ trailer that my dad handmade. The trailer is a restaurant on wheels with an oven, deep fryer, griddle, two grills, one flying pig shaped smoker, sink, hot water heater, prep area and propane tanks. Lindy Lou is the name of the flying pig and we joked with my dad that she was his mistress as he spent so much time and money on her. My goal is too feed the homeless at Pioneer Park at least once a month. Depending on donations sometimes we feed as many as three times. Lindy Lou was my dad’s pride and joy and I am thankful that I can carry on his memory with her.


Therapy Dog Team

Violet and I work almost daily on our manners and passing our Therapy Dog Team Test. We hope to be ready once Covid calms down. We want to visit retirement homes and be part of the READ! program. Violet has yet to meet a person, dog, or squirrel she doesn’t want to kiss and be loved on by. To prepare for this test we have visited many stores, parks, events, and even malls.
